Bachelor's Thesis

The goal of this work is to develop an adaptive animation system that selects the appropriate animations based on the situation and adjusts them at runtime. The animations are modified by the system using inverse kinematics. The system is divided into two main components: the player component, which manages the attack movements of the player character, and the opponent component, which handles the hit reactions of the enemy when struck by the playerâ€™s attack. To select animations for the player character, colliders are used to detect whether the target object is within them. The animations for reactions are defined for each attackable body part and adjusted based on the attack direction. An editor extension will allow developers to quickly and easily integrate this system into their own projects.
